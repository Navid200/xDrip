## Persistent High alert
[xDrip](../../README.md) >> [Features](../Features_page.md) >> [xDrip Alerts](../Alerts_page.md) >> Persistent High alert  
  
Glucose level alerts notify you when your glucose is either too low or too high. However, it’s not just the current glucose level that matters—how long your glucose remains high is equally important.  
  
For example, after meals, you may expect your glucose level to rise temporarily. However, if it stays high for an extended period, you need to be notified. This is where the Persistent High Alert feature becomes essential. It alerts you if your glucose level remains above a specified threshold for longer than a set duration.  
  
The Persistent High Alert continuously monitors your current and past glucose levels to determine how long you’ve been in a persistently high state. If necessary, it triggers an alert to help you take action.   
  
The image below shows the Persistent High Alert page, where you can configure the Persistent High Alert settings.  To access it, navigate to: `Settings` &#8722;> `Alarms and Alerts` &#8722;> `Extra Alerts` &#8722;> `Persistent High Alert`.      
![](./images/PersistentHigh.png)  
  
The alert is activated when your glucose level remains above the threshold (your set high level) continuously for longer than the duration specified under “For longer than (minutes).”  
<br/>  

---  
  
#### **Threshold**  
You can have xDrip use `Settings` &#8722;> `Glucose Units` &#8722;> `High Value` as the threshold for your Persistent High Alert by enabling `Threshold: High Value`.  

Or you can have xDrip use the threshold you specify on this page as the threshold for your Persistent High Alert independent of your High Value by disabling `Threshold: High Value`.
